Business Delivery Case Study

Turning ambiguity into a shippable release

My delivery playbook: align stakeholders, document requirements, write user stories + acceptance criteria, then run UAT to ensure release readiness.

Focus requirements Artifacts stories + AC Quality UAT readiness

1) Align on the decision

  • What problem are we solving and for whom?
  • What does “success” look like (measurable)?
  • What’s in scope vs out of scope?

Outputs

  • Scope statement + decision log
  • Process map (current → future)
  • KPIs and success criteria

2) Specify requirements

Make it unambiguous and testable.

User story template

As a [role],
I want [capability],
So that [business value].

Acceptance Criteria:
- Given [context], when [action], then [expected result]
- Include edge cases and error handling

Replace with your real (anonymized) story style.

Definition of Done

  • Acceptance criteria met + stakeholder sign-off
  • Validation checks complete (if reporting/data)
  • UAT passed; defects triaged
  • Release notes + training delivered

3) Test with intent (UAT)

UAT is where clarity becomes confidence.

UAT plan

  • Scenarios mapped to acceptance criteria
  • Expected results + validation steps
  • Defect severity and resolution workflow

Launch readiness

  • Stakeholder training + quick reference
  • Monitoring plan (what to watch after go-live)
  • Feedback loop + iteration plan

Shipping principle

“If it’s not testable, it’s not shippable.” Clear AC + UAT reduces rework and speeds delivery.
